# My Journey to the Hult Prize Idea

## Background

Throughout my life, I have always been curious and passionate about trying new things. I enjoy exploring different ideas, participating in competitions, and working on interesting projects. Competitions have always attracted me, but many times I couldn't participate because I didn't have a team.

## The Beginning of the Hult Prize Journey

Toward the end of 2025, the promotion for the **Hult Prize 2026** competition started at my university, Begum Rokeya University. I have always been interested in competitions, but usually I didn't participate due to the lack of a team.

One day, suddenly one of my friends suggested that we should participate in the competition. Interestingly, he had never shown much interest in such competitions before. When he said that, I told him to form a team. Honestly, I thought he might not actually do it and was just saying it casually.

The registration deadline for the **Hult Prize 2026 Begum Rokeya University campus round** was January 4. Unfortunately, we missed the deadline initially. Later we heard that the registration deadline had been extended, so we quickly completed our registration.

## Forming the Team

My friend brought two junior students from another department, and together we formed a team. At that time, the campus was closed due to the campus election, so all our team meetings were conducted online.

We started discussing what problem we should solve and what kind of idea we could develop for the competition.

## The Moment of Inspiration

One day, I was drinking coconut water from a street vendor. The vendor gave me a **plastic straw** to drink the coconut water. After finishing the drink, I threw the straw on the road without thinking much about it.

When I returned to my room, a thought suddenly came to my mind:

*"If everyone throws plastic straws like this everywhere, just like I did, how much damage could it cause to the environment?"*

This simple moment made me curious.

## Research and Realization

I searched online to learn more about plastic straw usage. During my research, I discovered something shocking:

**In the United States alone, about 500 million plastic straws are used every single day.**

I was truly surprised by this number. That moment made me realize how a very small daily habit can create a massive environmental impact.

And that was the moment when the seed of my idea began to form.
